# d12 x0 Re-Injection: Flat 1.0 Init

**Date:** 2026-03-19
**Branch:** `exp/attention_improvements`
**Hardware:** Single H100 80GB HBM3
**Wall time:** 35.03 min

## Summary

Tested whether the optimizer can discover a better x0 re-injection curve than the
hand-prescribed linear decay (0.20→0.05). Changed `x0_lambdas` init to flat 1.0
at every layer and let the optimizer learn the shape.

**Result:** Final BPB **0.8548** vs baseline **0.8538** (delta +0.0010, within noise).
The optimizer found a very different curve than the prescribed decay, but BPB was
essentially unchanged — the model compensates either way.

## Key Observations

1. **Not a simple decay.** The optimizer did NOT converge to anything like the
   prescribed 0.20→0.05 linear decay. The learned curve is jagged and non-monotonic.

2. **Layer 8 spike (2.1x).** The strongest re-injection is at L8, not at the
   beginning. This is near the "midpoint boost" pattern predicted in the handoff
   doc, shifted ~2 layers toward the end.

3. **High at L0 (1.67).** Model wants strong input signal at entry.

4. **Negative values at L9 (-0.54) and L11 (-0.91).** The model actively
   *subtracts* the input embedding at the final layers. This suggests the input
   signal becomes harmful to the output distribution near the end — the model
   wants to shed the raw embedding before the LM head.

5. **resid_lambdas all decreased** from their 1.15→1.05 init to the 0.42–0.95
   range. The model reduced residual stream strength across the board, with the
   strongest reduction in early layers (L1: 1.14→0.46, L2: 1.13→0.42).

6. **BPB matched baseline.** Starting from flat 1.0 neither helped nor hurt
   final BPB, suggesting the model can compensate for init choice. The interesting
   signal is the *shape* of the learned curve, not the final loss.

## Implications

- The prescribed 0.20→0.05 decay is not optimal but doesn't matter much — the
  optimizer compensates. This suggests x0 re-injection is a soft knob, not a
  critical bottleneck at d12 scale.

- The negative values at L9/L11 are noteworthy. If confirmed at d24, this could
  inform architectural choices: late layers should explicitly *not* see the
  input embedding.

- The L8 spike suggests mid-network signal refresh is useful, aligning with the
  AttnRes/DCA literature on depth signal dilution. But the effect is small enough
  that the simple scalar approach captures most of the benefit.

- **Next step:** Consider testing at d24 where depth signal dilution is more
  severe. The learned curve may be more impactful at greater depth.

## Training Stability

No loss spikes or instability despite the aggressive flat 1.0 init (5-8x higher
than default at most layers). The optimizer corrected smoothly from step 0.
