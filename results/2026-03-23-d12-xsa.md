## Analysis

1. **XSA-all (all 12 layers) shows a consistent BPB improvement of ~0.002-0.004** across training. The advantage emerged by step 300 and held steady through the end. Final improvement: -0.0024 BPB. This came at a cost of 3.3% throughput and 8.1% more memory.

2. **XSA-last4 (layers 8-11 only) shows a marginal improvement of ~0.0007 BPB.** The advantage was noisy early on but stabilized around -0.0007 from step 1200 onward. Throughput cost was minimal (1.9%) and memory overhead small (2.7%).

3. **At 124M scale, the XSA benefit is small but real** — consistent with the paper's note that gains are more pronounced at 0.7B+. The all-layers variant clearly outperforms last-4-only, suggesting that at this scale XSA benefits from being applied broadly rather than surgically.

4. **The throughput-adjusted picture:** XSA-all trains 3.4% slower but achieves the same BPB ~50-70 steps earlier (based on trajectory crossover). At this training length, the BPB gain outweighs the wall-clock cost. XSA-last4's throughput cost is negligible but so is its BPB gain.

5. **Baseline reproduced precisely:** final BPB 0.8539 vs prior 0.854 (delta: -0.0001), confirming experimental validity.
